Vegan Mapo Tofu

Description

This is a (vegan!) Mapo Tofu recipe that uses shiitake mushroom broth as its base. In place of pork, it uses crushed chickpeas.

Ingredients

  • 4 dried shiitake mushrooms
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • 3 sticks green onion
  • 1 cup canned chickpeas
  • 1lb soft or medium firm tofu
  • 2 pieces garlic
  • 1 small piece ginger
  • 1-2 Sichuan peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp chili oil
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 2 tbsp doubanjiang
  • 1 tsp cane sugar
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • Splash or rice vinegar
  • 2 tsp potato starch + 1 tbsp water

Instructions

  1. Place the dried shiitake mushrooms in a small saucepan and add in the water. Cover and bring to a boil.
  2. Chop the green onion. Roughly crush the chickpeas with a fork.
  3. When the water comes to a boil, simmer on medium for 10 minutes.
  4. Drain out the tofu and carefully slice into cubes.
  5. Pour out and reserve the mushroom water. Cool down the mushrooms with cold water and squeeze out the liquid. Then, remove the stalks from the mushrooms and finely dice.
  6. Finely chop the garlic and ginger.
  7. Heat up a saute pan to medium heat. Add the chili oil.
  8. Saute the chickpeas and mushrooms for 1-2 minutes. Add the garlic and giner and saute for another minute.
  9. Add in the chili powder, crushed pepper corn, and the doubanjiang. Give the pan a good stir, then add in the reserved mushroom water.
  10. Add in the cane sugar, soy sauce, and rice vinegar. Give the pan a good stir.
  11. Make a slurry by combining the potato starch with 1 tbsp water.
  12. Slowly pour in the slurry while stirring.
  13. Add in the tofu and gnently stir around them.
  14. Plate and garnish with the chopped green onion.
